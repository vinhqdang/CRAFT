\section{Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

The core philosophy of CRAF-X is to reject the premise of blind multi-modal aggregation. Instead of assuming the input modalities are inherently trustworthy, we implement a verify-then-fuse architecture. This verification occurs natively in the 3D Bird's-Eye View (BEV) space, allowing us to compute physical, spatial contradictions between the camera and LiDAR representations.

\subsection{Modality-Specific BEV Encoding}
Given $N_c$ perspective camera images $I \in \mathbb{R}^{N_c \times H \times W \times 3}$ and a LiDAR point cloud $P \in \mathbb{R}^{N_p \times 4}$ (XYZ and intensity), the first stage of the architecture extracts modality-specific BEV feature maps.

For the camera branch, we employ a Lift-Splat-Shoot (LSS) style view transform paired with a Swin-Transformer backbone. This generates a soft depth distribution per pixel, allowing the 2D image features to be projected into 3D frustum coordinates and voxelized into the BEV plane, yielding $F_{\text{cam}} \in \mathbb{R}^{H_b \times W_b \times C}$. Concurrently, the LiDAR branch processes the point cloud through a Sparse 3D Convolutional Network. The resulting 3D feature volume is height-collapsed via max-pooling to produce the LiDAR BEV representation $F_{\text{lid}} \in \mathbb{R}^{H_b \times W_b \times C}$.

\subsection{Cross-modal Consistency Probe (CCP)}
The CCP computes a per-cell consistency score $S(i,j) \in [0,1]$ that measures how semantically aligned $F_{\text{cam}}$ and $F_{\text{lid}}$ are at each specific BEV grid cell. Rather than performing global feature alignment, this module ensures strict local geometric agreement:
\begin{equation}
S(i,j) = \sigma\left( \text{MLP}_s\left( \left[F_{\text{cam}}(i,j) \oplus F_{\text{lid}}(i,j) \oplus |F_{\text{cam}}(i,j) - F_{\text{lid}}(i,j)|\right] \right) \right)
\end{equation}
where $\oplus$ denotes concatenation, $|\cdot|$ is the element-wise absolute difference, and $\sigma$ is the sigmoid activation function. When an adversarial perturbation displaces or corrupts camera BEV features relative to the uncompromised LiDAR geometry, $S(i,j)$ will drop significantly in the affected grid cells, explicitly tagging the spatial anomaly.

\subsection{Gated Adaptive Fusion Module (GAFM)}
The GAFM serves as the defensive quarantine mechanism. Using the consistency map $S$, it dynamically aggregates $F_{\text{cam}}$ and $F_{\text{lid}}$. We compute attribution maps via temperature scaling using hyper-parameters $\alpha$ and $\beta$, scaling the gate mechanisms based on the computed trust:
\begin{equation}
A(i,j,\text{cam}) = 1 - S(i,j)^\alpha  \cdot  \text{gate}_{\text{cam}}(i,j)
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
A(i,j,\text{lid}) = S(i,j)^\beta  \cdot  \text{gate}_{\text{lid}}(i,j)
\end{equation}

The final fused feature representation $F_{\text{fused}}$ guarantees that degraded or attacked interactions fall to an additive zero. If the spatial consistency indicates a perturbation, the corrupted modality is silenced for that specific grid cell:
\begin{equation}
F_{\text{fused}}(i,j) = A(i,j,\text{cam}) F_{\text{cam}}(i,j)  +  A(i,j,\text{lid}) F_{\text{lid}}(i,j) + \text{MLP}_{\text{cross}}( F_{\text{cam}} \otimes F_{\text{lid}} )
\end{equation}

\subsection{Adversarial Consistency Training (ACT)}
To ensure the spatial alignment functions as a robust attack anomaly detector, the entire module trains utilizing Projected Gradient Descent (PGD) over both modalities simultaneously. 
\begin{equation}
L_{act} = L_{det}^{\text{adv}} + \gamma \cdot \text{KL}( A_{\text{clean}} || A_{\text{perturbed}} )
\end{equation}
The Modal Attribution Regularization penalty ($\gamma \cdot \text{KL}$) guarantees that the spatial interpretation matrix $A$ responds faithfully, directly correlating to spatial injections occurring on individual modalities. This mechanism intrinsically binds the network's explainability to its mathematical robustness.

\begin{algorithm}[h]
\caption{CRAF-X Inference with Explainability Output}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Images $I$, LiDAR points $P$, threshold $\tau \in (0,1)$
\Ensure Detections $(H, B, V)$, Attribution $A$, Anomaly $S$
\State $F_{\text{cam}} \gets \text{CamEnc}(I; \theta_{\text{cam}})$
\State $F_{\text{lid}} \gets \text{LidEnc}(P; \theta_{\text{lid}})$
\State $S \gets \text{CCP}(F_{\text{cam}}, F_{\text{lid}}; \theta_{\text{ccp}})$ 
\State $A \gets \text{ComputeAttribution}(S)$
\ForAll{cells $(i,j)$}
    \If{$S(i,j) < \tau$}
        \State Disable cross-modal interaction for cell $(i,j)$
    \EndIf
\EndFor
\State $F_{\text{fused}} \gets \text{GAFM}(F_{\text{cam}}, F_{\text{lid}}, A; \theta_{\text{gafm}})$
\State $(H, B, V) \gets \text{DetectionHead}(F_{\text{fused}}; \theta_{\text{head}})$
\State \Return $(H, B, V), A, S$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
